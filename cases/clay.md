## English

### Case Status

standard

### Evidence Level

E3 strong

### One-Line Positioning

Clay turns go-to-market work into a table-first AI operating surface where teams enrich, research, score, personalize, route, and activate leads/accounts through columns, data providers, AI agents, and workflows.

### Business Scene

Sales / GTM / business automation, especially prospecting, account research, enrichment, outbound personalization, CRM operations, and signal-based workflows.

### Core Business Object

A lead/account table with rows, columns, enrichments, signals, AI research outputs, workflow steps, and destination systems.

The core object is a business data table that becomes both database and action surface.

### Production Artifact

Enriched lead/account lists, account intelligence, personalized outreach inputs, CRM updates, routing decisions, and GTM workflows.

### Operating Space

Table workspace.

Clay's product and docs center the table: users add people/companies, enrich rows through data providers, use formulas and AI agents, build waterfalls, and push results into outbound or CRM tools.

### AI Operation Model

AI acts as researcher, enricher, classifier, and personalization assistant.

Claygent and AI columns can research websites, summarize context, classify accounts, generate personalized text, and fill structured columns that downstream workflows can use.

### Human Control Model

Human control is column/workflow configuration.

The user defines rows, chooses enrichment sources, configures waterfalls, writes prompts/formulas, reviews generated cells, sets routing or export rules, and decides which records become actions.

### State And Memory

Clay preserves table rows, columns, enrichment history, provider results, waterfall steps, AI-generated cell values, CRM/outbound mappings, and workflow state.

### Delivery Boundary

The boundary is actionable GTM data: enriched records, prioritized accounts, personalized messaging inputs, CRM updates, or outbound workflows.

### Same-Scene Contrast

Clay differs from Quadratic by turning the table into an action workflow rather than a general analytical grid.

Quadratic is built around computation, code, and analysis. Clay is built around business data enrichment and activation: each column can represent a data source, AI step, decision rule, or action input.

### Reusable Pattern

Use the table as the operating surface for AI-powered business workflows.

The reusable structure is:

1. rows as business entities,
2. columns as actions/data fields,
3. enrichment providers as tools,
4. waterfalls as fallback logic,
5. AI columns as research/generation steps,
6. reviewable cell state,
7. exports/integrations as activation boundary.

### Reusable Recipe

1. Model each customer/business entity as a row.
2. Treat columns as both data fields and executable workflow steps.
3. Use waterfall logic to manage provider coverage and cost.
4. Let AI fill structured cells, not just free-form chat responses.
5. Make every generated field reviewable before activation.
6. Connect final table state to CRM, outbound, or operational systems.

### Inferred Pattern

- Clay's durable abstraction is the business entity row plus action columns.
- Its OS lesson is that AI business automation works best when AI outputs become structured, reviewable table state.
- Its tradeoff is data governance: enrichment power depends on provider quality, source transparency, permissioning, and compliance boundaries.

### Open Questions

- How visible is provenance for each enriched or AI-generated cell?
- How should teams control cost when waterfalls and AI research scale across large tables?
- Which workflow steps should remain human-reviewed before CRM/outbound activation?
